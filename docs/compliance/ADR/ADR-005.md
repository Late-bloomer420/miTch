# ADR-005: Pairwise DIDs over Pseudonymous Attestations

**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Status:** Accepted
**Deciders:** Jonas (Late-bloomer420)

---

## Context

To achieve unlinkability (GDPR Art. 5(1)(c), eIDAS 2.0 Art. 5a), the holder must present credentials without enabling cross-verifier correlation. Two main approaches exist:

| Approach | Issuer Roundtrip | Complexity | Unlinkability |
|----------|-----------------|------------|---------------|
| Pseudonymous Attestations (issuer mints fresh per-verifier credential) | Yes, per presentation | High | Strong (if issuer is honest) |
| Pairwise Ephemeral DIDs (holder derives per-verifier DID locally) | None | Low | Strong (no trust in issuer required) |

## Decision

Use **pairwise ephemeral DIDs** derived via HKDF from a master secret + verifier identifier. No issuer contact needed at presentation time.

Implementation: `src/packages/shared-crypto/src/pairwise-did.ts` (U-01)

- `did:peer:0z<multibase-encoded-public-key>` per verifier
- HKDF-SHA256 derivation: `masterSecret + verifierDID → per-verifier keypair`
- Key material shredded after each session (U-04)

## Consequences

**Positive:** Zero issuer dependency at presentation, issuer cannot track usage, simpler architecture, works offline.

**Negative:** Holder must protect master secret (device compromise = all DIDs derivable). BBS+ would provide stronger unlinkability but adds WASM dependency (deferred to U-10).

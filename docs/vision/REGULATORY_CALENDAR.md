# miTch — Regulatory Calendar & Go-to-Market Timeline

> Not implementation-ready. Thinking space only.
> Regulations are not just compliance burdens for others.
> They are the legal crowbars that force institutions to give users their data
> and the business development calendar for miTch.

---

## The Core Insight

Each regulation below does one or more of three things for miTch:

- **Creates a legal right** users can exercise (data portability, explanation, access)
- **Creates a compliance burden** institutions must solve (liability, audit, minimisation)
- **Creates a market** for tools that bridge the two

miTch sits at exactly that bridge.

---

## Timeline

### Already In Force

**GDPR — May 2018**
- Art. 20: Right to data portability — users can request their data in machine-readable format
- Art. 15: Right of access — users can see what data an institution holds
- Art. 17: Right to erasure — users can request deletion
- Art. 22: Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with significant effects
- Art. 5(1)(c): Data minimisation — institutions must collect only what is necessary
- **What it means for miTch:** The forgetting layer IS data minimisation compliance.
  Every institution that processes personal data has a legal obligation miTch helps them meet.
  The compliance burden is already there. miTch reduces it.

**PSD2 (revised Payment Services Directive) — 2019 (Open Banking APIs active)**
- EU banks are legally required to expose Open Banking APIs (Berlin Group standard)
- Third-party providers with AISP licence can pull transaction data on behalf of users
  with their explicit consent
- Authentication via OAuth2 / OpenID Connect using existing bank credentials
- Nordigen (GoCardless) provides AISP-licenced free-tier API access across EU banks
- **What it means for miTch:** The first real data connector is already legally mandated
  and technically available. Banking data portability doesn't require institutional cooperation —
  it requires an AISP licence. This is the first insight module data source.

---

### 2024

**EU Data Act — September 2025 (enforcement begins)**
- Right to access data generated by connected devices: car, smart home, appliances, wearables
- Manufacturers must provide access to the data their devices collect about users
- No institutional negotiation needed — it is legally required
- **What it means for miTch:** A whole category of data connectors (car, Fitbit, smart meter)
  becomes legally grounded without needing to negotiate with anyone.
  First devices likely targeted: fitness trackers (health data), smart energy meters (behaviour data).

**EU AI Act — August 2024 (phased enforcement starting 2025–2026)**
See dedicated section below. This is the most important regulation for the shadow profile problem.

---

### 2025

**EU AI Act — High-Risk AI Systems (enforcement begins ~August 2025)**
- Providers of high-risk AI systems must provide "meaningful information about the logic involved"
  in automated decisions that significantly affect individuals
- High-risk categories include: credit scoring, insurance risk assessment, employment decisions,
  access to healthcare, access to education
- Individuals have the right to request an explanation of decisions made about them
- **What it means for miTch:** See shadow profile section in SHADOW_PROFILES.md.
  This is the partial lever to make inferred profiles visible — from the output side.

**eIDAS 2.0 — EUDIW rollout begins**
- EU member states must offer a European Digital Identity Wallet to all citizens
- The wallet must be accepted by public services and large private platforms
- Note: current ARF has unlinkability issues (WIA device correlation problem) — do not depend on it
- **What it means for miTch:** When/if EUDIW stabilises, it becomes a potential identity bridge
  for data portability requests to any EU institution. Design miTch's auth layer so EUDIW
  can slot in without breaking the rest of the system. Don't build a hard dependency.

---

### 2025–2027

**European Health Data Space (EHDS) — phased rollout**
- EU regulation giving individuals electronic access to their health data across the EU
- "MyHealth@EU" patient summary: cross-border health record access
- Phase 1 (primary use, 2025–2026): patient access to own data from national health systems
- Phase 2 (secondary use, 2027+): research and health data sharing with consent
- Authentication via national eID (eIDAS 2.0)
- **What it means for miTch:** The health data connector becomes legally grounded as EHDS
  goes live. Each member state's rollout date is a go-to-market milestone for the health
  insight module. Austria (Elga) is already advanced — earlier opportunity.
  EHDS + miTch = user sees their health record + insight module shows what it means.

---

### 2025 onwards

**DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act) — financial sector, January 2025**
- Financial institutions must manage ICT risk, including third-party data processors
- Breach notification requirements tightened
- Data minimisation becomes directly tied to breach risk management
- **What it means for miTch:** Banks and insurers face new ICT risk scrutiny.
  Less data held = smaller breach surface = DORA compliance benefit.
  The forgetting layer as compliance middleware directly maps to DORA risk reduction.

**Data Governance Act (DGA) — September 2023, enforcement active**
- Creates a regulated category: "data intermediaries"
- Data intermediaries help individuals exercise their data rights and share data on their terms
- miTch is architecturally a data intermediary — could apply for official DGA status
- **What it means for miTch:** DGA status gives regulatory recognition, credibility with
  institutions, and a legal framework for the consent management and portability layer.
  Worth tracking. Application process is through national competent authorities.

## Austria-Specific (Innsbruck)

**Elga (Elektronische Gesundheitsakte)**
Austria's electronic health record system is already operational — ahead of most EU member states.
Patients can access their records via the Elga portal. Healthcare providers are connected.
This is the earliest real health data portability story in the EU.

A miTch health connector for Elga data is achievable before EHDS Phase 1 forces it everywhere.
Austria is the pilot market for the health insight module — ahead of schedule relative to other EU countries.

**Austrian Datenschutzbehörde (DSB)**
Austria's data protection authority. Files GDPR complaints through them.
The escalation path in the daily review links here for Austrian users.
URL: dsb.gv.at

**WKO Digital**
The Austrian Chamber of Commerce runs digital transformation programs.
Relevant for small business (pharmacy, retail) outreach and potential SME compliance tools.
